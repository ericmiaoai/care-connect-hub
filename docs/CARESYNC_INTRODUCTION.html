An introduction

An introduction · for everyone

Caring for someone you love shouldn't feel like running a small business.

A follow-up appointment that lives on a sticky note. A pharmacy run nobody's sure who's doing. A sister in Boston who keeps texting because she doesn't know what's happening. CareSync gives a family one shared picture — so caring for the person they love stops feeling like a logistics problem.

David had a heart attack three weeks ago. He's home now, recovering. His daughter Isla moved back temporarily. She's the one keeping the appointments straight, organizing pill boxes, reading the discharge papers.

Her brother Remy lives twenty minutes away. He helps when asked. Her sister Evie is in Boston with two kids. She calls every night, but she's never sure she has the full picture.

Four people. One person they all love. Six different apps trying to keep it together. Scroll down. ↓

Act I · A private place

Everyone who loves Dad. One sealed circle.

A Care Circle is the private workspace for one family caring for one person. Each member gets a role that fits their life.

Isla creates a circle and names it Dad's Care Team. CareSync generates a one-time invite link and a 4-digit PIN. She texts the link to Remy and Evie. They tap it, sign in, and enter the PIN. They're in.

Act IV · The voice in the room

When something matters, everyone hears it at once.

A broadcast is the one place in CareSync where the Admin's voice reaches the whole circle simultaneously.

It's Saturday. Dad's chest hurts. Isla calls 911. On the way to St. Mary's, she opens CareSync and posts:

R Remy Sees it on his lunch break. Drives over.
E Evie · Boston Putting her kids to bed. Reads it. Exhales. She doesn't have to call.

Only the Admin can broadcast. Tasks and appointments are collaborative; broadcasts stay focused — so when one arrives, everyone trusts it.

Act V · The quiet power

A photo of a discharge paper. Thirty seconds. A real appointment on the calendar.

Scan AVS is CareSync's AI feature. It captures the next follow-up appointment from any photographed medical paperwork — and only that — then drops it on the family calendar after a human confirms it's right.

Remy is at the hospital discharge desk. The nurse hands him three pages of paperwork. The follow-up cardiology date is on page two. He's tired. He doesn't want to type any of it. So he doesn't.

Gemini is fast. Gemini is not perfect. That's why every Scan AVS pipeline ends with a human review. The AI does the typing. The Admin does the trusting. Sensitive medical details never enter the database — only the next appointment does.

Access & limits. Scan AVS is available to Admins and Caregivers only — Viewers have read-only access and cannot trigger the AI pipeline. To keep usage sustainable, each user is limited to 10 scans per rolling 24-hour window. The counter is visible in the app header and updates in real time. Slots restore gradually as older scans age out of the window — there is no hard midnight reset.
